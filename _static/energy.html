Neighborhood Energy

Neighborhood energy systems produce steam, hot water or chilled water at a central plant and then pipe that energy out to buildings in the district for space heating, domestic hot water heating and air conditioning. Individual buildings don't need their own boilers or furnaces, cillers or air conditioners. A district energy system does that work for them.
